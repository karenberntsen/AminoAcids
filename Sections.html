Sections

The simplistic first assumption is that χ1 and χ2 of isoleucine have energy minima at 60, 180, and 300 degrees. Consequently, we have divided the distributions in nine sections with these values in the middle. The goal of this study was to find how the optimal torsion angles deviate from this simplistic assumption as function of parameters, such as secondary structure, accessibility, etc. and to find out how refinement software adds systematic deviations as function of, for example, data resolution. Every section represents a rotameric state of isoleucine and is therefore defined by its χ1 and χ2 torsion angles.

Section 1: χ1 = <0,120>, χ2 = <240,360>
Section 2: χ1 = <120,240>, χ2 = <240,360>
Section 3: χ1 = <240,360>, χ2 = <240,360>
Section 4: χ1 = <0,120>, χ2 = <120,240>
Section 5: χ1 = <120,240>, χ2 = <120,240>
Section 6: χ1 = <240,360>, χ2 = <120,240>
Section 7: χ1 = <0,120>, χ2 = <0,120>
Section 8: χ1 = <120,240>, χ2 = <0,120>
Section 9: χ1 = <240,360>, χ2 = <0,120>
