Help Scatterplots (counts or mean χ-value per resolution bin or per year)

Over the years, we learn more about proteins and about software. Consequently, X-ray refinement parameters can differ from year to year. In this section, we analyse how the χ1 and χ2 of isoleucine have changed over the years.
In the scatter plots, we use colours for secondary structure, as indicated on the individual pages. Mean values of both χ1 and χ2 are drawn and their corresponding standard deviation of the mean.
Resolution bin sizes have been 0.1 or 0.2 Å, unless indicated otherwise.
Please be aware that the vertical axes can either be linear or logarithmic. Further, 'variable y-range' or 'fixed y-range' indicates that plots have been individually optimised or that all plots in a section have the same y-axes. 50 observations are required for a point to be drawn and at least one drawn point is required for a plot to appear.
Although most plots are subdivided into nine sections, some plots are only limited to three sections, corresponding to the three rotameric states of one of the to χ angles, indicated by g+, t or g-.
g+: χ = <0,120>
t: χ = <120,240>
g-: χ = <240,360>

For some plots we subdivided the isoleucines in a loop by the conformation of their local backbone and named it after the corresponding secondary structure element. See Table 1 for the selection criteria. Since there are very few isoleucines in a loop with a backbone of a left-handed helix, we excluded these isoleucines from the plots.

Table 1: Ranges of φ and ψ to define the type of local backbone
Name Abbreviation min(φ) max(φ) min(ψ) max(ψ)
α-helix H -120 -40 -60 20
β-strand S -180 -40 100 180
left-handed helix L 40 120 -20 60
unknown U Everything else
